Ancient Greece

Overview

Ancient Greece was a civilization that flourished from the 8th century BCE to the Roman conquest in 146 BCE, influencing art, philosophy, and politics worldwide.

Philosophers

Greece was the birthplace of famous philosophers like Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle, whose ideas shaped Western thought.

Architecture

Greek architecture is known for its temples, such as the Parthenon, which showcase columns and balance in design.

Legacy

The legacy of Ancient Greece lives on through its contributions to democracy, literature, and the sciences.
