Ancient Mesopotamia

Overview

Ancient Mesopotamia, often called the "Cradle of Civilization," was located between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers and is known for the development of writing, law, and the first cities.

Inventions

Mesopotamians are credited with many important inventions, including the wheel, cuneiform writing, and the concept of time (the 60-minute hour).

Religion

Mesopotamians practiced polytheism, worshiping gods such as Anu, Enlil, and Ishtar. They built large temples called ziggurats to honor these deities.

Legacy

The advancements of Ancient Mesopotamia in writing, mathematics, and governance laid the foundation for future civilizations, influencing culture and society today.
